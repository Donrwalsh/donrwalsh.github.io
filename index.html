I have spent my whole life engaging with technology one way or another. When I was little I would spend hours tinkering and exploring. During the Great Recession I studied economics at university, paying my way by helping others solve their technology problems. After graduating I left the helpdesk behind to embark on a new adventure: Teaching myself how to code.

For half a year I delivered mail by day and tackled tutorials by night. I built my first website through pure trial, error and grit. I shared what I had built with the community and it was a hit! My site received over ten thousand visits that weekend and I was hooked. I dedicated as much time as I could to learning - getting married tends to distract - and soon began to write code professionally.

From those humble beginnings I've worked hard to amass seven years of professional experience. I got my feet wet at a startup then spent a few years as a technical consultant followed by a year and a half working as a staff application developer acting in a team lead capacity. I voluntarily left this last position to focus on some personal matters - getting divorced tends to distract - and now I'm refreshed and ready for the next step in this adventure.

Take a look at my Resume if you'd like to learn more about my professional experience. The CLRS section of this site contains much of my self-directed learning in the form of solutions to many exercises from the 3rd edition Introduction to Algorithms textbook by Cormen, Leiserson, Rivest and Stein. Thanks for visiting and I hope you enjoy learning more about me and my journey!
